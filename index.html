Day 2 · 24 May 2026 · Sunday

🎒 What to Carry: Waterproof phone pouch, sunscreen SPF50+, sunglasses, hat, reef shoes (rocky paths), towel, cash (~500 THB), water bottle, after-sun gel.
⚠️ Tan Alert: Avoid direct sun 11:30 AM – 2:30 PM! Stay in shade, snorkel, explore caves, or have lunch during peak hours. Reapply sunscreen every 90 min!

11:45 AM
🚶 Walk to Railay East + Explore the viewpoint trail (shaded jungle!)
💡 Railay East has the Viewpoint & Lagoon trail. 15-min steep muddy climb with ropes – adventurous but rewarding! Mostly shaded by jungle canopy.
12:30 – 01:30 PM
🍝 Lunch at Railay East (shaded restaurants — perfect for noon!)
🍴 Flame Tree Restaurant – great Thai food with cliff views (~150-300 THB/dish)
🍴 Mangrove Restaurant – right on the beach, seafood & Thai curries

03:30 PM
🚤 Return longtail boat to Ao Nang (~15 min, ~100-150 THB/person)

🏊 Relax at hotel pool / freshen up
06:30 PM
🍽️ Dinner – pick one!

🍴 Krua Thara – local seafood by the water, great value
🍴 Laughing Gecko – cozy garden restaurant, Thai & Western

09:00 PM

🍹 Center Point Bar – live bands, pool table, popular with travelers

Day 3 · 25 May 2026 · Monday
4 Island Tour | Stay: Centara Anda Dhevi
🏝️ 4 Islands Tour 🤿 Snorkeling 🏖️ Koh Poda & Tup Island 🛍️ Souvenir Shopping 📍 Krabi
👙 What to Wear: Swimsuit/bikini UNDER clothes (you'll be on a boat all day). Rash guard/UV top recommended for snorkeling (prevents sunburn + jellyfish). Light cover-up for the boat.
🎒 What to Carry: Waterproof phone pouch (MUST!), sunscreen SPF50+ (reapply every 90 min!), sunglasses with strap, hat, reef shoes, towel, dry bag for electronics, cash (~500 THB), water bottle, motion sickness pills (take 30 min before boat).
⚠️ Tan Alert: You'll be on water all day — UV reflects off the sea! Wear rash guard + reapply sunscreen constantly. Sit under boat shade during 11:30 AM – 2:30 PM.
07:00 – 09:30 AM
Wake up + Breakfast + Get ready
💡 Wear swimsuit under clothes. Pack: sunscreen, towel, waterproof phone pouch, water bottle, cash for snorkeling gear rental.
09:30 AM
🚤 4 Island Tour departs from Ao Nang
💡 Full-day longtail boat tour (~6 hours). Usually includes lunch. Confirm with operator.
10:00 AM
🏝️ Stop 1: Koh Poda – pristine white sand beach with stunning views
💡 Crystal clear water, perfect for swimming. Great photo spot with Koh Poda sign. ~45 min stop.
11:00 AM
🏝️ Stop 2: Tup Island (Chicken Island) – the famous sandbar!
💡 At low tide, a sandbar connects Tup Island to Chicken Island – you can WALK between islands! Check tide times. Chicken Island is named for its chicken-shaped rock. Amazing snorkeling here!
12:00 PM
🤿 Snorkeling stop – coral reefs & tropical fish
💡 Rent snorkeling gear if not provided (~100-150 THB). Look for clownfish (Nemo!), parrotfish, and sea urchins. Don't touch coral!
12:30 PM
🍽️ Lunch on the boat or beach (usually included)
01:30 PM
🏖️ Stop 3: Phra Nang Cave Beach – the crown jewel!
💡 Stunning limestone cliffs rising from turquoise water. Visit the Princess Cave (fertility shrine) at the far end. One of the world's most beautiful beaches! ~1 hour stop.
03:00 – 03:30 PM
🚤 Return to Ao Nang pier
04:00 PM
🚿 Back to hotel – shower & rest
04:30 PM
💆 Evening spa session – you deserve it after a day at sea!
💡 Try a Thai herbal compress massage (~400-600 THB). Great for sore muscles after swimming & snorkeling all day.
06:30 PM
🌅 Ao Nang sunset from the beach
💡 Last evening in Krabi! Soak it in. Great photo opportunity from the beach promenade.
07:00 PM
🍽️ Farewell Krabi Dinner – make it special!
🥩 Carnivore Steakhouse – great steaks & BBQ
🦐 The Grotto – fine dining by the cliffs
🍴 Lae Lay Grill – hilltop restaurant with panoramic sea views
💡 Lae Lay Grill has STUNNING sunset views – book a table for 6:30 PM to catch the golden hour. Slightly pricey but worth every Baht!
09:00 PM
🛍️ Last-minute Ao Nang shopping – souvenirs, Thai snacks, elephant pants!
💡 Bargain hard at souvenir shops! Start at 50% of asking price. Good buys: coconut oil, Thai spices, dried mango, fisherman pants.
10:00 PM
🧳 Pack for Phi Phi! (Early checkout tomorrow)
⚠️ Ferry pickup is ~7:45 AM tomorrow! Pack tonight. Keep ferry voucher accessible. Light bag recommended for Phi Phi (1 night only).
10:30 PM
😴 Bedtime – early wake-up tomorrow!
💡 Sleep by 10:30 PM → Wake 6 AM = ~7.5 hrs. Last night in Krabi! Tomorrow you're off to Phi Phi Island 🏝️

Day 5 · 27 May 2026 · Wednesday
Phi Phi → Phuket | Stay: Burasari Phuket
🏝️ Maya Bay 💎 Pileh Lagoon 🐒 Monkey Beach 🎋 Bamboo Island ⛴️ Ferry → Phuket 🎉 Bangla Road 📍 Phi Phi → Phuket
👙 What to Wear: Swimsuit/bikini ON (you're on a boat from 6:45 AM!). Rash guard/UV top for snorkeling. Bring a dry change of clothes in waterproof bag for the afternoon ferry + Phuket evening. Evening: nice casual for Bangla Road.
🎒 What to Carry: Waterproof phone pouch, dry bag (for dry clothes + electronics), sunscreen SPF50+, reef shoes, towel, cash (~1500 THB), snorkeling gear, GoPro, water bottle, ferry voucher. Keep a separate bag with evening clothes for Phuket.
⚠️ Tan Alert: Morning boat tour (6:45–11:30 AM) — the early hours are fine but UV increases after 10 AM on water. Wear rash guard + hat. You'll be indoors/travelling after noon so minimal risk.
05:30 AM
⏰ Wake up early – it's worth it!
💡 Early start = fewer crowds at Maya Bay. Wear swimsuit, pack sunscreen, waterproof pouch, towel, cash.
06:00 – 06:30 AM
🍳 Quick breakfast at resort
06:45 AM
🚤 Board longtail/speedboat for Phi Phi Island Tour
💡 Half-day tour (~4-5 hrs). Book the early morning slot for the best experience. Can book through resort or Klook.
07:15 AM
🏖️ Stop 1: Maya Bay – THE iconic beach!
💡 Featured in Leonardo DiCaprio's "The Beach" (2000). Limited to ~300 visitors at a time. Entry ~400 THB. NO swimming allowed (conservation) but you can walk the beach & take photos. The towering cliffs are breathtaking! Early morning = magical light & fewer people.
08:00 AM
💎 Stop 2: Pileh Lagoon – crystal emerald water!
💡 A hidden lagoon enclosed by limestone cliffs. The water is impossibly clear & turquoise. Swimming allowed here! One of the most beautiful spots in all of Thailand. Perfect for photos – the water glows green!
08:45 AM
🤿 Snorkeling stop near Phi Phi Leh
💡 Amazing coral reefs & tropical fish. Look for blacktip reef sharks (harmless!), sea turtles, and Nemo clownfish. Best visibility in the morning!
09:30 AM
🗿 Stop 3: Viking Cave (view from boat)
💡 Can't enter – but impressive from the water! Ancient cave paintings of boats on the walls. Locals harvest bird's nests here (for bird's nest soup – very expensive delicacy!).
10:00 AM
🐒 Stop 4: Monkey Beach – cheeky residents!
⚠️ Keep belongings secure – monkeys WILL grab phones, sunglasses, food! Don't feed them. Watch from the boat or wade in carefully. Fun to watch but keep your distance. ~20 min stop.
10:45 AM
🏝️ Stop 5: Bamboo Island (if included)
💡 Some tours include this pristine white sand island. Perfect for a final swim. Entry ~400 THB (sometimes included in tour).
11:30 AM
🚤 Return to resort
12:00 PM
🚿 Shower + pack bags + checkout
💡 Checkout and leave bags with reception. You have time for lunch before the ferry.
12:30 PM
🍽️ Lunch at Tonsai Village
🍴 Cosmic – great pizzas & Thai food
🍜 Garlic 1991 – popular Thai restaurant
01:30 PM
🚶 Walk to Tonsai Pier (take resort's free boat back first)
02:00 PM
⚠️ Check in at Andaman Wave Master Counter (30 min before!)
02:30 PM
⛴️ Ferry Tonsai Pier → Rassada Pier, Phuket
~04:30 PM
🚢 Arrive Rassada Pier, Phuket
04:30 – 05:15 PM
🚗 Transfer to Burasari Phuket, Patong (~45 min)
💡 Pre-book a taxi or use Grab app. Rassada Pier → Patong is about 40-50 min. Cost ~600-800 THB by taxi.
05:30 PM
🏨 Check-in at Burasari Phuket
💡 Located right near Bangla Road & Patong Beach – perfect location! Freshen up, rest your legs after a big day.
06:30 PM
🏖️ Quick walk to Patong Beach – catch the sunset!
💡 Patong Beach is just 5 min walk from Burasari. Beautiful wide beach – great first impression of Phuket!
07:30 PM
🍽️ Welcome-to-Phuket Dinner!
🍴 No.6 Restaurant – legendary Patong street food, huge portions, super cheap!
🦐 Briley Seafood – fresh seafood, local favorite
🍴 Ali Baba Restaurant – if you're craving something familiar (halal options)

09:00 PM
🎉 Bangla Road – Phuket's LEGENDARY walking street!
💡 The road closes to traffic after 6 PM and transforms into a neon-lit party strip. Street performers, live music, bars, clubs everywhere. Just walk through and soak it in – no pressure to enter anywhere!
09:30 PM
🍹 Grab drinks at a rooftop bar
🍹 Illuzion Club – massive nightclub, big DJ nights
🍹 Seduction Beach Club – pool party vibes
🍹 Molly Malone's – chill Irish pub, live music, good for a relaxed drink
💡 Bangla Road bars often have free entry but drink prices vary. Buckets (cocktail buckets) are the iconic Bangla drink ~200-300 THB. Stay safe – watch your belongings!
10:30 PM
💆 Late night Thai massage on Bangla Road
💡 Tons of massage shops open late around Bangla. ~300-400 THB for a foot massage – perfect way to end a long travel day!
11:30 PM
😴 Bedtime – first night in Phuket!
💡 Sleep by 11:30 PM → Wake 7:30 AM = ~8 hrs. Big sightseeing day tomorrow – Big Buddha, Wat Chalong, Old Town & sunset at Promthep Cape!

Day 6 · 28 May 2026 · Thursday
Phuket Exploration | Stay: Burasari Phuket
🛕 Big Buddha 🛕 Wat Chalong 🏘️ Old Phuket Town 🏖️ Kata Beach 🌅 Promthep Cape Sunset 📍 Phuket
👕 What to Wear: Temple dress code! Cover shoulders & knees (both men & women). Men: T-shirt + long pants or knee-length shorts. Women: maxi dress or top + long skirt/pants. Carry a sarong/scarf to cover up. Change to swimsuit at Kata Beach later. Evening: nice casual for farewell dinner.
🎒 What to Carry: Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat, water bottle, camera, comfy walking shoes (lots of walking at temples + Old Town), power bank, cash (~1500 THB), sarong/scarf (temple cover-up + beach), swimsuit in bag (for Kata Beach stop).
⚠️ Tan Alert: Morning is temple visits (indoors/shaded). Kata Beach visit is scheduled for late afternoon (3:30 PM+) — after peak sun! Promthep Cape sunset is evening. Minimal sun exposure today.
07:30 – 09:30 AM
Wake up + Breakfast + Get ready
💡 Dress modestly today – you're visiting temples! Cover shoulders & knees. Carry a light scarf/sarong just in case. Wear comfy walking shoes.
09:30 AM
🚕 Grab taxi/Grab for the day (~1,500-2,000 THB)
💡 Negotiate a full-day rate with a taxi driver – cheaper than multiple Grab rides. Ask hotel reception to help arrange. Route: Big Buddha → Wat Chalong → Old Town → Kata Beach → Promthep Cape.
10:00 AM
� Stop 1: Big Buddha (Phra Phutthamingmongkol Akenakkiri)
💡 45-meter tall white marble Maravija Buddha sitting atop Nakkerd Hills (400m above sea level). Breathtaking 360° panoramic views of Phuket – you can see Kata, Karon, Chalong Bay & more!
💡 Free entry (donations welcome). ~30-45 min visit.
💡 Dress code strictly enforced – shoulders & knees must be covered. Free sarongs available at entrance if needed.
💡 Best photos: stand at the viewing platform behind the statue for the bay view!
📍 Open in Maps
11:00 AM
🛕 Stop 2: Wat Chalong (Chalong Temple)
💡 The most important and visited temple in Phuket. Beautiful Thai architecture with ornate pagodas and golden stupas.
💡 Free entry. ~30-45 min visit.
💡 The Grand Pagoda has a fragment of Lord Buddha's bone! Climb to the top floor for views.
💡 Remove shoes before entering any building. Don't point feet at Buddha statues.
💡 ~15 min drive from Big Buddha.
📍 Open in Maps
12:00 PM
🏘️ Stop 3: Old Phuket Town
💡 Charming Sino-Portuguese colonial architecture from the tin mining era. Colorful shophouses, street art, indie cafes & galleries.
💡 Must-see streets:
🎨 Soi Romanee – THE most photogenic street! Pastel-colored buildings, Instagram paradise.
🏛️ Thalang Road – the main heritage street with local shops & museums.
�️ Phang Nga Road – beautiful murals & street art on the walls.
💡 ~30 min drive from Wat Chalong. Plan ~1-1.5 hrs to explore & take photos.
📍 Open in Maps
01:30 PM
🍽️ Lunch in Old Town – tons of great options!
🍴 Tu Kab Khao – beautiful heritage house, amazing Southern Thai food (top rated!)
☕ One Chun – local favorite, authentic Phuketian cuisine
🍜 Raya Restaurant – classic old-town restaurant in a gorgeous mansion
💡 Tu Kab Khao is consistently rated one of the best restaurants in Phuket. Try the crab curry & Phuket-style Hokkien noodles!
03:00 PM
🚗 Drive to Kata Beach (~30 min from Old Town)
03:30 – 05:30 PM
🏖️ Kata Beach – relax, swim & soak up the sun
💡 One of Phuket's prettiest beaches! Less crowded than Patong, softer sand, great for swimming. Rent a sunbed (~200 THB) and chill.
05:45 PM
🚗 Drive to Promthep Cape (~15 min from Kata)

📍 Must-See Places

Ao Nang Beach, Krabi

The main beach hub of Krabi. Great for sunset walks, shopping, and catching boats to Railay and the islands.

Railay Beach

Accessible only by boat, surrounded by towering limestone cliffs. One of Thailand's most stunning beaches. Don't miss Phra Nang Cave Beach!

4 Islands Tour, Krabi

Visit Koh Poda, Tup Island (sand bar connecting islands!), Chicken Island, and Phra Nang Cave. Crystal clear waters for snorkeling.

Phi Phi Islands

Paradise islands with turquoise water, dramatic cliffs, and vibrant marine life. The iconic Maya Bay from "The Beach" movie.

Maya Bay

The world-famous beach from Leonardo DiCaprio's "The Beach". Limited daily visitors – arrive early! Entry: ~400 THB.

Big Buddha, Phuket

45-meter tall marble Maravija Buddha sitting atop Nakkerd Hills. Breathtaking 360° views of Phuket. Free entry. Dress modestly.

Kata Beach & Promthep Cape

Beautiful beach for relaxing. Promthep Cape offers Phuket's most spectacular sunset viewpoint – arrive early!

Bangla Road, Patong

Phuket's famous nightlife strip in Patong. Comes alive after 9 PM with neon lights, street performers, bars and clubs.

Old Phuket Town

Charming Sino-Portuguese architecture, colorful streets (Soi Romanee!), local cafes, and street art. Perfect for photos!

Wat Chalong Temple

Phuket's most important and largest Buddhist temple. Stunning architecture, peaceful grounds. Free entry. Dress modestly (cover shoulders & knees).

Phi Phi Viewpoint

A 20-30 min steep hike rewarded with THE iconic view of Phi Phi's twin bays. Best at sunset! Entry: ~30 THB. Bring water!

Pileh Lagoon

A hidden emerald-green lagoon enclosed by towering limestone cliffs. One of the most beautiful spots in Thailand – swim in crystal-clear water!

Bamboo Island

A small flat island with powdery white sand beach and amazing snorkeling. Part of the Phi Phi tour – perfect for relaxing between stops.

Promthep Cape Sunset

Phuket's most famous sunset viewpoint at the southern tip. Arrive 30 min before sunset for best spots. Stunning panoramic ocean views!

Patong Beach

Phuket's busiest and most popular beach – 3.5 km of sand. Great for water sports, beach chairs, and people watching. Walk here from your hotel!

Krabi Night Markets

Ao Nang night market & walking streets with cheap local food, clothes, souvenirs. Try the street Pad Thai (~40-60 THB) and fresh fruit shakes!

Loh Dalum Bay & Fire Shows

Phi Phi's famous party beach. Every night features FREE fire dancing shows on the sand from ~9:30 PM. Grab a bucket cocktail and enjoy!

💡 Travel Tips & Important Info

💰

Currency & Money

Thai Baht (THB). ~1 AED = 9.5 THB. ATMs charge 220 THB fee. Exchange at airport or SuperRich in town for better rates. Carry cash – small shops don't take cards.

📱

SIM Card

Buy at Phuket Airport arrivals. AIS or True Move – ~300-400 THB for 8-day tourist pack with unlimited data. Works everywhere including islands.

🕐

Time Zone

Thailand = UTC+7 (ICT). Same as Vietnam/Indonesia. UAE is UTC+4, so Thailand is 3 hours AHEAD of Abu Dhabi.

🔌

Power/Plugs

Thailand uses Type A, B, C plugs (220V). UAE plugs (Type G) need an adapter! Buy a universal adapter before the trip.

🌡️

Weather (May)

Start of monsoon season. Expect hot (30-33°C) with occasional rain showers. Carry a light rain jacket. Rain usually short and passes quickly.

🚕

Transport

Use Grab app (like Uber) in Phuket/Krabi. Negotiate tuk-tuk prices BEFORE getting in. Songthaew (shared truck) is cheapest. Longtail boats: negotiate!

🙏

Temple Etiquette

Cover shoulders and knees at temples. Remove shoes before entering. Don't point feet at Buddha images. Don't touch monks (especially women).

🍜

Food Tips

Street food is amazing and safe! "Mai pet" = not spicy. Try Pad Thai, Tom Yum, Green Curry, Mango Sticky Rice. Drink bottled water only.

💊

Health & Safety

Carry sunscreen (SPF50+), mosquito repellent, basic meds (paracetamol, anti-diarrhea). Reef-safe sunscreen for snorkeling!

🤿

Snorkeling Tips

Best visibility in morning. Wear reef shoes. Don't touch coral! Apply sunscreen 30 min before entering water. Waterproof phone pouch is a must.

⛴️

Ferry Tips

Sit on the upper deck for views but prepare for sun. Take motion sickness pills if needed. Keep valuables in waterproof bag. Arrive early!

👑

Respect the King

Thailand has strict lèse-majesté laws. Never disrespect the royal family. Stand for the royal anthem in cinemas. It's taken very seriously.
